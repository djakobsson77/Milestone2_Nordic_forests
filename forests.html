National parks and reserves

Stenshuvud national park

Stenshuvud is a nature reserve in Simrishamn municipality in Skåne County. The area has been protected since 2003 and is 8 hectares in size. The reserve is located just south of Stenshuvud National Park and includes an area of ​​deciduous forest around the northern Rörum River. Coastal mountains, lush deciduous forests, pastoral heaths and beautiful beaches combine in a magnificent landscape in Skånska Österlen. The national park has a southern exotic character and great biodiversity worth preserving and visiting.

Abisko national park

Abisko National Park is one of Sweden's national parks and is located near the small town of Abisko south of Torneträsk in northern Lapland. The park was established in 1909 and covers approximately 7,700 hectares. Mighty mountains frame the beautiful Abisko Valley with its flowering alpine meadows, beautiful rapids and Scandinavia's largest mountain lake. In summer you can get light 24 hours a day and see the midnight sun.

Tiveden national park

Tiveden National Park is a national park located in the Tiveden forest area. It was inaugurated in 1983 and covers approximately 20 km² of central Tiveden with Trollkyrka, Stenkälla cave, Vitsands beach and areas of untouched forest land. Tiveden offers interesting nature experiences and a strong wilderness feeling right between the cultural areas of Vänern and Vättern. Here, in one of southern Sweden's wildest forests, you can hike in chaotic boulder fields with up to 10-meter-high boulders that have been brought in by the ice sheet. There are also many exciting caves and several beautiful wilderness lakes.

Kosterhavet national park

Kosterhavet National Park is Sweden's first marine national park. It is located off the coast of Bohuslän in Sweden and covers an area of ​​38,878 hectares around the Kosteröarna archipelago within the municipalities of Strömstad and Tanum in Västra Götaland County. The park borders Ytre Hvaler National Park on the Norwegian side. Here you will find marine environments and species that are unique to Sweden, including the Kosterrännan with its steeply sloping rock walls and corals. Unique plant and animal life above and below the water offers experiences all year round. It is attractive for coastal hiking, canoeing, diving, swimming and other nature experiences both along the mainland and in the outermost sea belt.

Njupeskär waterfall

Njupeskärsfallet is one of Sweden's highest and most visited waterfalls, with a total height of 93 meters (70 meters free fall), located in Fulufjället National Park in northwestern Dalarna. It is known for its dramatic gorge, unique flora and an accessible hiking trail of about 4 km round trip. In the rapids mist next to the falls are many unusual mosses, lichens and vascular plants. Many of them require constant moisture to survive. The deep groove that has cut into the sandstone layers at the waterfall is a textbook example of backward erosion.
